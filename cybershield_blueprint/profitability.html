Y1 EBITDA Margin Clarification

Conclusion: Y1 EBITDA is a temporary high because salary is deferred, while steady-state margins appear only after compensation normalization.

What this means: The Year 1 EBITDA profile is useful for cash survival planning but not for long-term margin expectations.

  • Key numbers: Y1 EBITDA margin is 51% with zero salary cost.
  • Key numbers: Founder salary begins at JOD 1,500/month from Month 24.
  • Key numbers: EBITDA normalizes to 22–25% in Year 3 after salary normalization.
  • Key numbers: EBITDA recovers to 60–73% in Years 4–5 as revenue scales faster than costs.
  • Decision implication: Use Year 3+ EBITDA bands for valuation, pricing guardrails, and hiring cadence decisions.

09.4

Margin Improvement Drivers Over Time

Year 1–2: Lean Phase

Driver: Zero staff cost. Low platform cost. Revenue per client improves as mix shifts from Lite to Standard/Pro.

Risk: Founder burnout. Single incident can disrupt all clients simultaneously.

Key metric to track: Hours per client per month. Must stay below 5h average.

Year 3–4: Hiring Dip

Driver: Revenue scaling faster than headcount. Each new hire adds JOD 8,000–12,000/year but enables JOD 50,000–80,000 in additional client revenue.

Risk: Hiring too early or too late. Each 3-month hire delay = JOD 1,500–2,500 saved but burnout risk increases.

Key metric to track: Revenue per employee.

Year 5: Leverage Phase

Driver: Fixed cost platform serves 84 clients at similar infra cost as 20 clients (storage aside). Automation matures — analyst time per client drops further.

Risk: Client concentration (no single client >15% of revenue by Y5).

Key metric to track: NRR (Net Revenue Retention) — target >110% via upsells.
