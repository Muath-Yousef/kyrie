02.2

Recommendation: IFZA Dubai + Jordan Ops

Recommended: IFZA (International Freezone Authority) — Dubai

IFZA gives a UAE legal presence, bank-account access, and market credibility without full UAE operating overhead. In this plan, it is the lowest-friction path to enter UAE contracts while keeping delivery cost anchored in Jordan.

IFZA Specific Details

  • License type: Professional Services / Cybersecurity Consulting
  • Activities: IT Consulting, Cybersecurity Services, GRC Advisory, Managed Security Services
  • Visa eligibility: Yes (1 visa with standard license)
  • UAE bank: Emirates NBD, RAK Bank, or Mashreq business account
  • Annual cost: JOD 3,510 license + JOD 410 virtual office = JOD 3,920/year
  • Formation agent: Virtuzone (~JOD 545 one-time fee)
  • Timeline: 5–10 business days for license issuance

Jordan Entity (Complementary)

  • Register as sole proprietorship under Ministry of Industry and Trade (MOI)
  • Activity: “IT and Technology Consulting Services”
  • Cost: JOD 150–200 one-time
  • Purpose: Issue invoices for Jordan-based clients, track local expenses
  • Tax: Register with Income & Sales Tax Dept (ISTD). Founder is consultant to UAE entity.
  • Accounting: Monthly JO bookkeeping ~JOD 40/month
  • Important: UAE entity invoices UAE clients. Jordan entity invoices Jordan clients.
UAE Corporate Tax (9%) — Freezone Qualifying Conditions

As of June 2023, UAE imposes 9% corporate tax on taxable income exceeding AED 375,000 (~JOD 102K). Freezone entities may qualify for 0% on “Qualifying Income” if: (1) income derives from non-UAE mainland clients, (2) the entity meets “Adequate Substance” criteria. If the majority of clients are UAE mainland SMBs, the 9% rate likely applies. Budget accordingly. At JOD 558K Y5 revenue, tax provision: ~JOD 60,825 (modeled in financials). Engage a UAE-qualified tax advisor annually (JOD 300–500/year).

Final Decision Logic

Hybrid is superior for this blueprint because it combines UAE revenue access with Jordan cost control. Jordan-only makes sense when capital is very limited and the goal is to validate operations before UAE expansion. UAE-only makes sense only when funded to absorb higher fixed costs and targeting larger, compliance-heavy contracts from day one.

Sales Angle: Compliance = Legal Obligation = Faster Close

When a prospect says “we don’t know if we need cybersecurity,” ask: “Does your company handle customer data in the UAE? Do you process payments? Are you in healthcare or finance?” If yes to any — NCA ECC 2.0 applies. UAE PDPL applies. Frame every sale around legal obligation first, then technical value. This reduces objection cycles by 2–4 weeks.

Competitive White Space: JOD 1,500–8,000 / Year SMB Segment

The JOD 1,500–8,000/year SMB cybersecurity budget range in the UAE is structurally underserved. Enterprise MSSPs cannot profitably descend to this price band. Generic IT MSPs lack true SOC capability. Offshore providers lack the regional compliance credibility that UAE procurement decisions increasingly require. This gap is the primary commercial opportunity — and it is compliance-driven, which means it is durable and growing as NCA ECC 2.0 enforcement matures.

Competitive Risk: Easy to Enter, Hard to Defend at Scale

The open-source SIEM/SOAR stack (Wazuh + Shuffle) is not proprietary. A competitor can replicate the technical architecture. The durable moat is not the tooling — it is operational playbooks, client relationships, compliance expertise depth, and automation maturity built over 2–3 years. Invest in documentation, SOP quality, and client retention from day one. The business that executes best, not the one with the best tools, wins this segment.
