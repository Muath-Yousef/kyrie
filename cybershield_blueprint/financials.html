07.1

Model Assumptions

Conclusion: The model assumes controlled growth, lean fixed spending, and variable costs that rise only with client and log-volume expansion.

Revenue Assumptions

  • Client acquisition pace is conservative in Year 1 and scales only after reference wins.
  • New clients/quarter: Q1–Q4 = 2 each, Q5–Q8 = 3 each, Q9–Q12 = 4–6, Q13–Q16 = 6–7, Q17–Q20 = 7–8.
  • Annual churn assumption: 10% (modeled as 2.5%/quarter after Year 1).
  • Blended AACV rises from JOD 3,500 (Y1) to JOD 5,800 (Y5) as mix shifts to higher tiers.
  • Revenue mix target: 70% subscriptions, 20% advisory, 10% project work.
  • Cash collection delay assumption: 45 days blended (monthly + annual contracts).

Cost Assumptions

  • Scalable Costs (per client/log volume) rise from JOD 900/Q to JOD 13,500/Q as active clients expand.
  • Hiring is trigger-based: no salary load until workload requires it (Q8 onward in base case).
  • Staff cost profile: JOD 0 through Q8, then JOD 1,875/Q, reaching JOD 8,250/Q by Y5.
  • Operating overhead grows with scale from JOD 2,175/Q to JOD 5,875/Q.
  • UAE license + virtual-office commitments are fixed and included each quarter.
  • Tax model uses UAE CIT at 9% on positive taxable income.

Key Model Updates vs Original

  • ✔ CAC assumption of JOD 480 is embedded in marketing + sales line items.
  • ✔ Churn is applied quarterly after Year 1 instead of ignored.
  • ✔ Payment delay is modeled in cash flow, not hidden in optimistic timing.
  • ✔ Deferred revenue logic is explicit for annual prepay contracts.
  • ✔ Founder salary begins after break-even to show true steady-state margin.
Break-Even Logic

Conclusion: Break-even is achieved once retained recurring clients can absorb fixed burn and variable delivery costs.

What this means: Signed deals matter less than retained active clients, because retained subscriptions are what convert the business to positive cash.

  • Key numbers: Break-even quarter is Q8 (Month 22–24).
  • Key numbers: Expected break-even client count is 18–24 active clients.
  • Key numbers: Break-even requires recurring subscription revenue to cover Monthly Burn (Fixed) and Scalable Costs (per client/log volume).
  • Decision implication: Track retained active clients as the primary KPI, not only quarterly logos signed.

Y1 EBITDA 51% — Context

Conclusion: Y1 margins are temporarily inflated by deferred founder compensation, not by true steady-state efficiency.

What this means: The 51% Y1 EBITDA is a transitional accounting result and should not be used as an ongoing operating margin target.

  • Key numbers: Y1 EBITDA margin is 51%.
  • Key numbers: Founder salary is deferred in Y1 and starts after break-even.
  • Key numbers: Margin compression occurs during salary normalization, then improves again with scale and automation.
  • Decision implication: Use post-normalization margin years for planning headcount, compensation, and investor messaging.

Worst Case Survival Strategy

If growth is at -50% pace: (1) Immediately activate Jordan market with lower pricing to build MRR base. (2) Pause all paid marketing. (3) Offer 3-month trial contracts instead of annual to accelerate closure. (4) Add one-time project services (pentests, ISO consulting) to bridge cashflow. (5) Delay first hire until Month 30+. The business remains viable but takes 3.5 years to reach break-even instead of 2 years.
