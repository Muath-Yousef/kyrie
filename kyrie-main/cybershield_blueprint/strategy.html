SLA Penalty / Remedy Clause

Include in all MSAs: “In the event of SLA breach, Client is entitled to a service credit equal to 5% of monthly subscription fee per breach, up to a maximum of 15% of the monthly fee. Service credits are the sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches and do not entitle Client to terminate the agreement.” This is standard practice — protects you from unreasonable claims while demonstrating accountability.

11.4

Service Exclusions & Professional Boundaries

Why Explicit Exclusions Matter

Without explicit exclusions, clients assume everything is included. Scope creep is the second most common MSSP margin killer after log cost overruns. Every MSA must clearly define what is not included.

General Service Exclusions (All Tiers)

  • No guarantee of breach prevention — monitoring and detection services only
  • No remediation of third-party or unmanaged systems unless explicitly agreed and scoped
  • On-site physical presence excluded unless specified in contract (premium add-on)
  • Patch installation and system remediation remain with Client’s IT team unless contracted
  • Legal or regulatory compliance opinions are advisory only — not formal legal counsel
  • Services performed only on systems explicitly listed in the asset inventory at contract signing
  • Detection quality is dependent on availability and quality of telemetry provided by Client
  • No liability for incidents caused by Client-side misconfigurations or unauthorized changes
  • Availability SLAs do not apply during scheduled maintenance windows (notified 48h in advance)

Tier-Specific Exclusions

  • SOC Lite: No after-hours human response. Automated alerting only outside business hours. No GRC, no threat hunting, no cloud coverage. IR limited to 3 events/year.
  • SOC Standard: No dedicated named analyst. No on-site. GRC not included (separate service). IR limited to remote-only, 10 events/year.
  • SOC Pro: On-site IR limited to 2 visits/year maximum. Cloud coverage requires cloud credentials access agreement.
  • Pentest (all): Scope strictly limited to assets in agreed Rules of Engagement. No unauthorized access to out-of-scope systems. No destructive testing unless explicitly agreed. Findings are advisory — remediation is Client’s responsibility.
  • GRC / vCISO: Not a substitute for formal legal or regulatory compliance counsel. Recommendations are advisory. Audit outcomes are not guaranteed.

Log Overage & Data Management Boundaries

Log Volume Caps

Each contract tier specifies maximum log ingestion volume per day. Volumes exceeding the contracted cap are throttled at the collection layer. Overage charges: JOD 0.20–0.50/GB/day depending on tier. Weekly volume report provided. Client is responsible for configuring log sources within contracted limits.

Data Retention

Hot (searchable) data retained per tier. Cold archive retained per compliance requirements or MSA. On termination: Client data deleted within 30 days (or transferred on written request + export fee). No guarantee of data recovery after retention period expiry.

Data Residency

Standard service: Hetzner EU (Falkenstein, Germany) — GDPR compliant. UAE data residency available at premium (Azure UAE North or AWS UAE). Client must specify requirement before contract signing. Post-signature migration charged separately.

Year 2–5 Strategic Growth Plan

Year 2–3 Priorities

  • Microsoft MISA Partnership: Apply for Microsoft Intelligent Security Association. Adds credibility and access to Microsoft’s partner referral ecosystem for Sentinel-adjacent clients.
  • Wazuh Partner Program: Official partner status — brand credibility + potential ecosystem leads.
  • ISO 27001 Managed Service product: Monthly compliance retainer model (not one-time project). Easier to budget for clients + stickier than project work.
  • Jordan market activation: CBJ-regulated banks, JIC insurance clients, MoH healthcare entities. JOD pricing highly competitive. Use as MRR floor while UAE pipeline matures.
  • Incident Response retainer product: 20h/year IR retainer at JOD 1,500–3,500. Pure margin — rarely fully consumed. High-margin MRR add-on.

Year 3–4 Priorities

  • CREST Membership (Year 3): Required for enterprise pentest credibility in GCC. Budget JOD 1,500 for application and annual fees. Opens enterprise segment.
  • SOC 2 Type II Audit: Budget JOD 4,000–6,000. Required for US-operated clients. Opens EU/US client segment for remote delivery.
  • White-label Synapse Core: License the SOC platform to 2–3 Jordan IT firms who lack security capability. JOD 500–800/month per white-label partner. Pure margin, zero new client acquisition.
  • UAE physical presence: Flexi-desk at IFZA or Dubai co-working (JOD 200–350/month) — needed for larger client meetings and government-adjacent prospects.

Year 4–5 KSA Expansion

Saudi Vision 2030 + NCA KSA mandates create the largest SMB cybersecurity demand in the region. KSA entry strategy:

  • Correct registration path: Register as a foreign company branch via Saudi Arabia’s Ministry of Investment (MISA). Requires a local service agent (not equity partner). Process takes 4–8 weeks, fees ~JOD 1,500–2,500. Alternatively, a channel-first model (Saudi reseller partner, 20–25% commission) avoids registration for initial market testing.
  • Same remote delivery model from Jordan — no KSA office required initially
  • Partner with 1–2 Saudi IT distributors as channel (reseller model, Year 4)
  • Target: 5–10 KSA clients by Year 5, contributing JOD 60,000–80,000 additional ARR
  • NCA KSA ECC + PDPL-KSA compliance angle — same expertise, different regulation

Bahrain + Kuwait — Adjacent Markets

Compliance-driven GCC markets accessible via the UAE entity without additional registration in most cases:

  • Bahrain: Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) cybersecurity framework (2022) is actively enforced across licensed financial entities. Bahrain FinTech Bay ecosystem (300+ firms) is compliance-hungry. CBB rulebook volume 5 requires incident management, access controls, and security testing. Accessible market: 200+ regulated financial entities + logistics sector.
  • Kuwait: CITRA (Communications and Information Technology Regulatory Authority) cybersecurity regulations expanding post-2022. Less mature enforcement, but growing market with significant oil-sector IT budget. 3–6 month longer sales cycle vs Bahrain.
  • Both markets: same Arabic/Gulf cultural dynamics as UAE. Partner channel approach (local IT reseller, 20% commission) is the lowest-friction entry without new entity registration.
  • Target: 3–5 clients per market by Year 5 via partner channel

Raise & Scale

Pre-seed or seed round ($500K–1M) from a MENA-focused VC or strategic investor. Use capital to hire faster, enter KSA, and pursue SOC 2 + CREST simultaneously.

Trigger: Pursue this only if organic growth exceeds base case by 30%+ in Year 2–3. Do not raise capital to solve an execution problem.

11.7

Final Strategic Verdict

This Business Is Viable — Execute With Discipline

The market is real and underserved. The technology is proven and affordable. The cost arbitrage (Jordan operations + UAE billing) is structural and durable. The automation model is architecturally sound. The three-layer commercial structure (assessments → subscriptions → advisory) is the correct motion for UAE SMB sales.

The primary risks are not market or technical. They are execution risks: maintaining service quality during solo Year 1–2 operations, controlling SIEM data costs, surviving the 18–24 month client acquisition ramp, and not burning out before the first hire.

With JOD 18,000–22,000 in launch capital and 12 months of patient, disciplined execution, break-even is achievable by Month 22–24. Year 3 and beyond represent a genuinely profitable, scalable security business with strong exit optionality.
