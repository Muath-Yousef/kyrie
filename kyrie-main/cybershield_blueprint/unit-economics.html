Pre-Filtering / Detection Pipeline (Reduces Volume)

Log Filtering Strategy

Not all logs need full ingestion. Implement a lightweight detection pipeline before the SIEM that filters noise:

  • Filebeat pre-filter: Drop repetitive successful auth events (keep failures only)
  • Rate limiting: Cap verbose IIS/Apache access logs at 5% sampling (full logs go to cold archive, not hot SIEM)
  • Risk scoring pre-filter: Only forward events with risk score ≥ 3 to active SIEM correlation engine
  • Deduplication: Wazuh's built-in deduplication removes repeated identical events within 60-second windows
  • Result: Effective daily ingest reduced by 40–60% vs raw volume cap

Multi-Tenant Isolation Architecture

Each client is a separate tenant — critical for security and compliance:

  • Elasticsearch: Separate index per client (e.g., client-acme-*) — never shared indexes
  • Wazuh: Separate Wazuh manager group per client with RBAC role
  • TheHive: Separate organization per client — analysts see only their client's cases
  • SOAR (Shuffle): Separate workflow namespace per client
  • Dashboards: Kibana/Grafana spaces — client-specific views if portal access provided
  • Data residency: All on Hetzner EU (Falkenstein, Germany) — GDPR compliant

Combined, this creates margins 20–30 percentage points above industry average MSSPs who pay for Splunk/QRadar licensing.

06.4

Payment Delay Impact on Cashflow

Conclusion: Payment timing, not booked revenue, is often the deciding factor for early-stage survival.

UAE Payment Delay Reality — 30 to 90 Days is Normal

What this means: In UAE B2B, cash collection lag is structural, so runway planning must prioritize cash timing rather than invoice totals.

  • Key numbers: Typical UAE SMB payment delay is 30–90 days.
  • Key numbers: Government payment timing can extend to 90–120 days.
  • Key numbers: Delay pressure is highest in Year 1 while Monthly Burn (Fixed) is being funded from limited reserves.
  • Decision implication: Default contracts to annual prepay or partial upfront structures whenever possible.

Scenario: Annual Prepay (Best)

What this means: Annual prepay maximizes predictability because cash is received before delivery while revenue is recognized over time.

  • Key numbers: Upfront payment is JOD 4,600 for SOC Standard annual.
  • Key numbers: Cash arrives in Month 1 with zero payment delay.
  • Key numbers: Revenue recognition remains JOD 383/month over 12 months.
  • Decision implication: Use annual prepay discounts to reduce working-capital risk early.

Action: Offer 12% discount for annual prepay. This is worth 12% of revenue to have zero cashflow uncertainty.

Scenario: Monthly Invoice (Typical)

What this means: Monthly billing introduces a predictable receivables gap that must be funded from reserves.

  • Key numbers: Invoiced on day 1 and paid on day 45 under 15+30 terms.
  • Key numbers: Effective service-before-cash gap is 1.5 months.
  • Key numbers: At 10 clients × JOD 420/month, about JOD 6,300 remains outstanding.
  • Decision implication: Improve invoicing cadence and collection workflow before scaling monthly contracts.

Action: Issue invoices on 25th of month (due 30th of following month). Use UAE business bank account for faster ACH.

Scenario: 90-Day Enterprise Terms

What this means: Net-90 terms can distort cash conversion so severely that payment structure must be negotiated, not accepted by default.

  • Key numbers: Enterprise clients may request Net 90 terms.
  • Key numbers: Annual structure target: 50% upfront + 50% at Month 6.
  • Key numbers: Project structure target: 50% upfront + 50% on delivery.
  • Decision implication: Reject Net-90 monthly subscriptions and enforce staged payment milestones in the MSA.

Action: Include payment terms explicitly in MSA. Late payment fee: 2%/month on outstanding amounts.
