10.1

Operational Risks

HIGH
Solo Founder Key Person Risk
All service delivery, sales, and operations depend on one person in Year 1–2. Illness, burnout, or emergency halts all 20+ client services simultaneously. This is the single highest-consequence risk.

Mitigation: (1) Maintain SOPs for every process from Day 1 in Notion. (2) Identify trusted freelance backup analyst (JOD 150–200/month part-time retainer from Month 6). (3) Purchase personal income protection insurance (~JOD 25/month). (4) Hire trigger set at 60+ hours/week for 3+ weeks, regardless of client count. (5) Annual resilience test: can your platform run 48h without you?
HIGH
Client Acquisition Rate Below Projection
UAE B2B security sales cycles average 3–9 months. If acquisition rate is 50% of base case, Year 1 revenue drops to JOD 12,000–16,000, delaying break-even to Month 30–36.

Mitigation: (1) Secure 2–3 design partner clients (Jordan SMBs with UAE operations) at 30% discount before full launch — builds references. (2) Never depend solely on UAE cold outreach — activate warm network first. (3) Maintain 8-month runway cash reserve. (4) Jordan clients as baseline MRR while UAE pipeline develops. (5) Offer quarterly-first contracts to accelerate closure.
HIGH
SIEM Data Cost Overrun
A single misconfigured client logging verbose access logs can generate 50–200GB/day. At Hetzner storage rates, this is manageable. But if the stack migrates to Sentinel or Elastic Cloud, costs multiply 8–15x. Uncontrolled log volume bloats infra costs and analyst triage time.

Mitigation: (1) Hard log caps enforced at Filebeat level — client cannot exceed contracted GB/day without automatic throttle. (2) MSA includes explicit overage clause (JOD 0.20–0.50/GB/day over cap). (3) Weekly ingestion monitoring report per client. (4) Client onboarding includes "log hygiene" session — configure sources correctly from Day 1.
MED
Own Infrastructure Security Breach
CyberShield's SIEM infrastructure holds security telemetry for all clients. A breach of this infrastructure exposes every client simultaneously — a catastrophic reputational and legal event. The irony of an MSSP being breached is commercially fatal.

Mitigation: (1) Zero-trust access model — all admin via YubiKey + VPN. (2) Wazuh agent on own servers (monitor yourself). (3) Annual pentest of own infra (JOD 1,000–2,000). (4) Cyber liability insurance from Day 1 (JOD 600–1,000/year). (5) Client data disclosure procedure: 4h SLA. (6) Multi-tenant isolation: per-client index segregation always enforced.
MED
Premature Client Onboarding / Poor Quality
Signing clients before Phase 0 is complete means deploying to real production environments with untested playbooks. A false negative (missed real attack) in first 90 days could end a client relationship and generate negative word-of-mouth in the small UAE SMB community.

Mitigation: (1) Never accept paying clients until Phase 0 metrics are met (85%+ automation, <15% false positive rate, 10+ playbooks tested). (2) First 2–3 clients: offer reduced pricing in exchange for feedback tolerance. (3) Over-invest in first client onboarding — 8 hours minimum, not 4.
MED
UAE Analyst Talent Retention
Jordan-based analysts with 2+ years of real MSSP experience become highly attractive to EU/US remote employers at 3–5x Jordan salary. Losing a trained analyst costs 3 months of transition + JOD 1,800–2,100 in rehire/training costs, and temporarily degrades service quality across all clients.

Mitigation: Market-rate salary, certification support, performance bonuses, clear promotion track. Target 2+ year tenure per hire. Cross-train all staff on all clients — no single analyst is the only one who knows a client's environment.
10.2

Financial Risks

HIGH
High Churn Rate (>15%) in Years 1–2
Early churn from poor onboarding, missed SLAs, or perceived low value is doubly damaging: lost MRR + reputational damage in the small UAE SMB community. The market is small enough that 2–3 negative references from early clients can significantly slow acquisition.

Mitigation: Over-invest in first 5 clients. Monthly check-in calls. Annual Business Review (ABR) presentation before contract renewal. Renewal discount (5%) for early sign. SLA adherence score tracked and shared with client monthly. Quantify value in every report: "We handled 487 alerts this month. Here are the 3 that required action."
MED
UAE Corporate Tax — Freezone Qualifying Conditions
UAE 9% CIT applies from June 2023. Freezone "qualifying income" exemption requires meeting specific criteria. If the majority of clients are UAE mainland SMBs, freezone protection may not apply to all revenue. At Year 5 revenues of JOD 558K, a 9% tax on full income = JOD 50,220/year — not catastrophic but must be planned.

Mitigation: Engage UAE-qualified tax advisor annually (JOD 300–500/year). Model 9% CIT on all income (as done in our projections — fully modeled, not ignored).
MED
Jordan Income Tax on UAE-Sourced Income
Jordanian residents are taxed on worldwide income. Sole proprietor income from the UAE entity may be subject to Jordan income tax (5–20% graduated rate). Double-taxation without proper structuring reduces net income significantly.

Mitigation: Structure founder as documented consultant to UAE entity at agreed market rate (JOD 1,500–2,000/month). Keep Jordan entity separate for Jordan-only transactions. Annual Jordan tax advisory: JOD 300–600/year. Do not begin billing until this structure is confirmed by Jordan-licensed tax advisor.
LOW
JOD/AED/USD Currency Exposure
JOD pegged to USD at 0.709. AED pegged to USD at 3.67. De-facto JOD/AED rate is stable at ~3.68. USD-denominated tools (Hetzner invoices in EUR, Cloudflare in USD) create minor FX exposure but represent <15% of costs.
10.3

Strategic Risks

MED
UAE Data Residency Requirements
Some UAE regulations (NCA, NESA, HAAD) require data to remain within UAE borders. Hetzner cloud is EU-based. For clients in regulated sectors (healthcare, banking, government-adjacent), this could be a disqualifying requirement.

Mitigation: (1) Clarify data residency requirements in client discovery call. (2) For regulated clients requiring UAE data residency: offer Microsoft Azure UAE North (Dubai) or AWS UAE (Dubai) hosted SIEM at a premium tier price. (3) Include data residency clause options in MSA.
MED
Open-Source Tool Maturity / Vendor Risk
Wazuh and Shuffle are actively maintained but community-supported. A critical security vulnerability in the SIEM itself, or a major breaking release, could require emergency migration. Wazuh has had critical CVEs in the past (e.g., agent code execution vulnerabilities).

Mitigation: All deployments containerized (Docker Compose) for rapid re-deployment. Version pinning strategy — never auto-update production. Test releases in staging first. Subscribe to Wazuh security advisories. Backups restore-tested monthly. Consider Wazuh paid support plan (~$3,000/year) when client base exceeds 50.
LOW
Larger Competitor Enters UAE SMB Market
A regional MSSP could launch a lower-cost SMB tier. However, structural cost advantage (Jordan ops + open-source stack) is difficult to replicate quickly. Client relationships and trust built over 2+ years are not easily displaced by price alone.
