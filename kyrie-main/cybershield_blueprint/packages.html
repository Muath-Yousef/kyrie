04.5

Sales Playbook — GRC-First Motion

Do NOT sell SOC directly. Sell the pain first.

The expert review recommends: Lead with GRC Assessment as entry point. It's faster to close (compliance = legal obligation), establishes credibility, and naturally surfaces the need for SOC monitoring. The correct sales sequence for UAE SMBs is assessment → gap → retainer → SOC → upsell.

Correct Sales Sequence

Step 1 · Entry
Free NCA Gap Check or Paid Assessment
Offer a 30-min free NCA ECC gap walk-through (no tools, just conversation + checklist). Or a paid Baseline/Email/Website Assessment at JOD 350–800. Creates urgency based on real gaps found. No commitment required from client.
Step 2 · Deliver
Gap Report Presentation (30–45 min call)
Present findings. Quantify risk in business terms: "Your email domain has no DMARC — any attacker can spoof your CEO's email to your finance team. In UAE, BEC fraud costs SMBs an average of AED 180,000 per incident." Show regulatory obligation (NCA/PDPL). End with: "Here's what a monthly retainer looks like to close these gaps."
Step 3 · Propose
Synapse Guard or Governance Proposal
Send a clean 2-page PDF proposal within 24 hours of gap call. Include: scope, monthly/annual pricing, SLA, what's included/excluded, 3 case study references (even if anonymized). Subject line: "Security Program for [Company] — Proposal." Follow up in 3 days if no response.
Step 4 · Objection Handling
Common Objections + Responses
"Too expensive" → "Compare to one incident: AED 50,000–200,000 recovery cost. This is JOD 590/month."
"We have IT" → "IT manages your systems. We monitor for threats your IT team can't see — 85,000 new malware samples daily."
"Not sure we need it" → "NCA compliance is mandatory for your sector. We can start with just the email and web protection at JOD 300/month."
"Let us think about it" → "Of course. Shall I hold the discounted annual rate for 2 weeks? I'll send a reminder in 5 days."
Step 5 · Close
MSA + SLA + First Invoice
Sign MSA (Master Service Agreement) — have this ready as a PDF. SLA attached as exhibit. First invoice: annual prepay (preferred) or first 3 months upfront. DocuSign or printed + scanned accepted.

LTV:CAC of 40:1 is Exceptional

SaaS benchmark for a "healthy" business is 3:1. A 40:1 ratio means every JOD 1 spent acquiring a client returns JOD 40 in lifetime value. This is possible because: (1) annual contracts = low churn, (2) low CAC via relationships and referrals, (3) high AACV relative to acquisition cost. Protect this ratio by maintaining client retention above 85%.
