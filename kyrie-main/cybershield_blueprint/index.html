Executive Summary

This is a decision-grade feasibility and execution blueprint for a Managed Security Service Provider (MSSP) focused on UAE SMBs. The operating model is hybrid: a UAE legal entity for market access and trust, with delivery anchored in Jordan for cost control.

The service portfolio is built in three layers: entry assessments, recurring SOC subscriptions, and advisory projects. This structure moves clients from low-friction first contracts to long-term recurring revenue while protecting gross margin throughout the growth curve.

Core Thesis

UAE SMBs face real compliance pressure (NCA ECC, NESA, ADHICS, PDPL) but cannot absorb enterprise MSSP pricing. An automation-first model using open-source SIEM/SOAR delivers required security outcomes at materially lower COGS than commercial alternatives. The core business logic is operational leverage: build an automation engine, then package it as managed security services at SMB-accessible pricing, generating EBITDA from the cost gap.

01.4

Phase 0 · Operator Validation

Required Pre-Launch Step: Internal SOC Validation

Before acquiring real paying clients, the SOC platform must be validated under near-real conditions. Phase 0 is a 4–8 week internal exercise with 2–3 volunteer or heavily discounted pilot clients. No commercial sales until all pass criteria are met.

Phase 0 Objectives

  • Deploy Wazuh + Shuffle on Hetzner — connect 2–3 real environments
  • Measure: alerts/day, false positive rate, response time, SOAR automation %
  • Build and test 10 core playbooks (phishing, brute force, malware, recon, etc.)
  • Establish multi-tenant isolation (index separation + RBAC)
  • Write SOPs for onboarding, escalation, reporting, off-boarding
  • Validate: can 1 operator handle 3 clients without overload?

Structural Moat: Automation × Arbitrage × Compliance Depth

The competitive position is not based on lower price alone. It is the combination of automation-driven COGS reduction (≥80% alert automation eliminating analyst overhead at scale), Jordan talent cost arbitrage (SOC analyst at JOD 700–1,000/month vs AED 12,000+ in UAE), and UAE compliance depth (NCA ECC 2.0, PDPL, ADHICS, DFSA expertise) that enterprise MSSPs cannot replicate without destroying their margin model, and that generic IT MSPs cannot replicate without building the capability from scratch. This is a durable, structural competitive position — not a temporary pricing gap.
